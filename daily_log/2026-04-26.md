## Decisions and direction

Phase 11 is a continuation of the IR lowering roadmap that started at Phase 6 (function calls) and progressed through Phase 10 (control flow). The unary expression lowering is a relatively small addition — two operators, one match arm, four tests — but it signals that the backend is now moving through expression-level constructs rather than statement-level control flow. The remaining unsupported expression kinds in `lower.rs` (ArrayLit, Index, MethodCall, StructLit, HandleVal, HandleDrop, Range) are the next frontier.

The negate implementation choice — lowering `-x` to `0 - x` rather than a dedicated `Neg` instruction — is pragmatic. It reuses the existing `Binary::Sub` infrastructure and avoids expanding the IR instruction set. This is consistent with how many compilers handle unary negate at the IR level.

The cargo test fix is maintenance but tells us something about the state of the test infrastructure: the warning cleanup sprint was aggressive enough to break the test suite, and the fix was targeted and clean. The 123-pass/1-fail result confirms the pre-existing IR backend test failure is still there — likely the same one that has been tracked since Phase 10.
