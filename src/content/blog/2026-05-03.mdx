## PR #53: Documenting unary lowering

Commit `2d665a4` added 25 lines of block-level comments to `src/ir/lower.rs` explaining the encoding choice for unary operations. The short version: the IR has no dedicated unary instructions. `Op::Minus` lowers to `0 - value` (subtraction from zero), and `Op::Not` lowers to `value == 0` (equality check against zero).

This is a deliberate design decision. By representing all operations as two-operand forms, every backend only needs to handle one instruction shape. No special cases for negation or boolean inversion. The tradeoff is a slightly less obvious IR for anyone reading raw output, but the backend simplification is worth it for a language at this stage.

PR #53 was created yesterday and merged into submain today. No behavioral change, just recording the reasoning in-code where future contributors will find it.
